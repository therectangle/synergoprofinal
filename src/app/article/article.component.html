Blue light: debunked

Download Article Share

What is blue light?

Blue light is a high intensity light at the extreme range of the light spectrum which is visible to the human eye. Due to its high intensity, blue light is harmful to your eyes as it passes directly through the cornea into the retina.

So, is blue light blue in color?

Yes, but not always. Even white light contains a significant range of blue light components.

Is all blue light harmful to the eyes?

The impact of blue light on your eye health depends on 2 factors:

1. The amount of blue light emitted from the light source.

2. The duration of exposure to the blue light.

Light sources that emit more blue light

Arc lamps (E.g. welding torch)

Sun (staring directly)

Dental treatment lights

Bright white light

Light sources that emit less blue light

Fluorescent lamps and LED lights

Digital Screens (Smartphones, Laptops, etc.)

Who is at risk and why?

Working adults Exposed to a digital screen for prolonged periods at work. (Laptops, Smartphone, etc.) and outside work.

Kids and Teenagers Extensive usage of mobile phone, watching TV, etc.

Elderly 50 years and above are highly susceptible to age-related macular degeneration. Blue light accelerates this process.

Harmful effects of blue light
SHORT TERM EXPOSURE

Difficulty in falling asleep due to a decrease in Melatonin (sleep hormone) secretion.

Feeling lethargic and fatigue.

LONG TERM EXPOSURE

Retinal damage

Cataract formation

Accelerates rate of macular degeneration

Chronic fatigue and deficiency in sleep

Tips to manage blue light risk

Use night shift/night light mode in smartphones and laptops to reduce blue light emission.

Consider using yellow tinted glasses if you use your computer at night.

Avoid screen usage 2 - 3 hours before bedtime to prevent melatonin suppression and get a more restful sleep.

Ensure your room is completely dark for sleeping, use an eye mask and ear plugs to minimise disruptions.

Consider using warm (yellow) lights at home and dim red lights as night light.

Limit blue light exposure from mobile devices with the 20-20-20 rule

Every 20 minutes look at something 20 feet away for 20s. to relax your eyes.

Or close your eyes/stare into space for 20s.

Try looking at distant greenery for a more calming effect to the eyes.
